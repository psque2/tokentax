## The Tax Loss Harvesting Thing — Actually Useful

Tax loss harvesting gets thrown around as a buzzword, but TokenTax has a real tool for it. The dashboard scans your current holdings, identifies which assets are sitting at an unrealized loss, and shows you exactly how much you could offset against your capital gains by selling them.

There's no annual cap on offsetting capital gains this way (though $3,000/year is the limit for offsetting ordinary income). For an active trader with a mixed bag of winners and losers, this alone can save you more than the cost of the subscription.

## How the Integrations Work

TokenTax connects to 100+ exchanges and wallets through read-only APIs — meaning it can view your transaction history but can't touch your funds. Supported platforms include Coinbase, Binance, Kraken, BitMEX, MetaMask, Phantom, Aave, Compound, and a long list of others.

For anything not directly supported, you can upload a CSV file of your transaction history. It's a bit more manual, but it works.

The platform generates several tax forms automatically:
- IRS Form 8949 (capital gains and losses)
- Schedule D
- Mining and staking income reports
- ETH gas fee reports
- FBAR (foreign bank and financial accounts)
- Audit trail reports

One-click export to TurboTax, H&R Block, and TaxAct is built in.

## A Note on 2026 Tax Season

The IRS opened the 2026 filing season on January 26, 2026, and the deadline is April 15, 2026. Extensions are available (pushing the deadline to October 15), but taxes owed are still due April 15.

Two changes matter for crypto holders this year: centralized exchanges like Coinbase and Kraken may now send you Form 1099-DA for the first time, reporting gross proceeds. And the IRS now requires wallet-by-wallet cost basis tracking — you can no longer pool holdings across platforms. That makes accurate software more important than it was even a year ago.
